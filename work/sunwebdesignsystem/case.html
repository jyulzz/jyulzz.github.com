Context

The Sunweb Group1 is an organization operating 14 tourism brands in 10 European countries.

Across 7 main offices, over 500 people collaborate to build products for each of the group's brands, using a wide range of tools, methods, systems and languages.

In Q2 2017, the UX team in Amsterdam, Netherlands (of which I was a part of) and a Front-end Development team in Girona, Spain started a conversation on issues faced in keeping product designs consistent using current workflows.

Our shared pain point was that without a common, comprehensive and up-to-date style reference, it was difficult to build a consistent user experience across the wide range of products offered to customers under each brand.

A related concern was the productivity lost in correcting discrepancies in designs between products and in searching for design elements without a clear source to work from.

It quickly became clear that offering everyone in the company access to a Syleguide-type resource documenting each brand’s visual properties and product components would be the most efficient way to improve both the quality of products for final users and our productivity.

Summary

Role

  • Prepare and participate in brainstorming sessions,
  • Co-author the Design System's information architecture,
  • Design the main UI container and UI elements,
  • Conduct weekly progress update meetings with developers,
  • Coordinate with representatives from Marketing and Creative departments.

Deliverable

A Single Source of Truth2 Design System, containing all visual properties and product components for each of the group’s 14 brands, accessible to anyone in the company.

Team

  • 8 developers,
  • 2 designers (including me),
  • 2 department representatives from Creative and Marketing departments.

Results

  • Empowerment of each employee with direct access to valid, up-to-date design resources,
  • Improvements in visual consistency across products and media within each brand,
  • Streamlining of the design process and elimination of negative dependencies.

Process

Research

3 rounds of brainstorming sessions were held between the 2 founding teams, focused on the vision, the principles of execution and the objectives of the project.

I scheduled and lead additional meetings with Marketing and Creative department members to discuss their needs in terms of content to be included in the Styleguide (e.g: the Creative Department asked for video resources to be included in brand assets, such as TV idents to be used in commercials).

Planning

Based on research input, essential features needed to produce an MVP3 were shortlisted.

The Design System should:

  1. be accessible by anyone in the company,
  2. contain all style elements for each of the brands, with at minimum : Foundations, Material Elements, Color Schemes, Components and Media Resources,
  3. be consumable through an API4, allowing more rapid product development and integrations into other systems within the company.

Production

We started designing the first wireframes, sharing them with developers and other future users of the system.

With these first designs delivered, implementation by developers began.

Testing

Successive private releases of the Design System were used in real conditions for the design of new products across the company.

The benefits were immediate, encouraging us in expanding the scope of the system and enriching it with new content.

Final Thoughts

Being part of such a large-scope and foundational effort is an honor. The benefits for the company are both immediate and long-term, strengthening the internal design culture as well as empowering designers, developers, marketers and everyone else with the same level of access to the visual DNA of each brand, in a format useful to all, reducing negative dependencies, clearing bottlenecks in worfklows and making everyone's job easier by sharing productive information.
